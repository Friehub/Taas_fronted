# TaaS Tokenomics

The TaaS economy is designed to reward node operators for **Attestation Accuracy**, **Availability**, and **Scalability**, while providing predictable costs for massive data consumers.

## The Pillars of the TaaS Economy

### 1. Proof of Stake (Security)
To ensure the network is resistant to Sybil attacks, node operators must stake **TAAS tokens**.
- **Collateral-Backed Truth**: Every truth point proposed by a node is backed by its stake. 
- **Slashing**: If an operator is proven to have provided malicious, fraudulent, or inconsistent data, a portion of their stake is "slashed" (burned or redistributed), ensuring economic alignment with the protocol's integrity.

### 2. Multi-Tier Fee Model
TaaS supports diverse consumer needs through a two-track revenue system:
- **Standard (Pay-As-You-Go)**: Individual developers pay a small micro-payment in TAAS or Stablecoins per truth point.
- **Enterprise (SLA-Based)**: High-volume platforms (like Polymarket) can utilize monthly subscriptions. This track includes "Priority Lanes" for guaranteed low-latency resolution during peak market volatility.

### 4. Revenue Distribution (The Fee Split)
Fees collected for truth resolution are automatically split across the network's contributors:
- **Truth Nodes (50%)**: Rewards for logic execution and P2P consensus.
- **Gateway Hive (30%)**: Rewards for secure data fetching and TSS signing.
- **DAO Treasury (15%)**: Funding for protocol development and network growth.
- **Referrer/Platform (5%)**: Incentives for SDK integration and market creation.

## The TAAS Token Utility

The TAAS token is the backbone of the decentralized ecosystem:
1. **Governance**: Vote on new data sources, fee adjustments, and protocol upgrades (RIPs).
2. **Payment Gas**: The primary currency for querying the network.
3. **Incentive Alignment**: Fee splits and staking rewards are denominated in TAAS.

By tying economic success to data accuracy, TaaS creates a self-sustaining ecosystem that rewards honest participants and penalizes bad actors.
